01 · Project Overview

Teaching children about money — through play

Fubon Finance commissioned a financial education app for children aged 4–12 — a category that barely existed in Taiwan at the time. The challenge wasn't just building something "for kids." It was building a product that served two completely different audiences simultaneously: children who needed delight, game mechanics, and age-appropriate complexity; and parents who needed confidence, control, and transparency.

A purely child-facing app would fail parents. A purely parent-facing one would bore children instantly. The design had to hold both — feeling like a game to a 7-year-old and a financial dashboard to their parent, all within the same product.

02 · Who Are the Users?

Two audiences. One seamless ecosystem.

The app operates as a shared ecosystem where both users are active but playing entirely different roles. Understanding what each user type actually wanted — not just what the client assumed they wanted — was foundational to every design decision.

Parents
Admin & Educator
Primary goal Educate and accumulate savings for the family
  • Create & assign tasks to children
  • Monitor consumption and savings history
  • Approve high-value reward redemptions
  • Share strategies via social platform
Kids
Player & Learner
Primary goal Earn points, complete missions, reach wishes
  • Finish parent-assigned tasks
  • Unlock & complete game missions
  • Manage a personal wish list & allowance
  • Redeem points for gifts or charitable giving
03 · User Motivations

What drives each user to open the app?

Understanding motivations — not just tasks — is what separates a product people use from one people enjoy. Both audiences needed a reason to return daily, but those reasons are fundamentally different.

Parents Educate children & accumulate family savings

Parents are motivated by long-term outcome — they open the app to see progress, assign new challenges, and feel confident that their child is developing healthy financial habits. The social sharing element also lets them connect with other parents navigating the same journey.

Kids Play, earn, and make their wishes come true

Children are motivated by immediate feedback and visible progress toward something they care about. Five distinct motivation levers were identified:

  • Accomplish game missions and unlock new challenges
  • Cultivate their own financial knowledge through play
  • Earn and manage a personal allowance (Yoyo Pay)
  • Turn good habits into consistent, rewarded behaviour
  • Exchange knowledge and discoveries via the social platform
04 · Product Architecture

Three interconnected feature pillars

The product logic is organised around three core value streams — each addressing a different layer of the family finance experience. They are designed to reinforce each other: completing tasks earns points, points build savings, and savings connect to real goals on the wish list.

Task & Habit Cultivation

Turns everyday responsibilities into a reward loop — linking behaviour to learning outcomes.

Behavioural design principle

The reward loop is intentionally short for younger children (visual, instant) and progressively longer for older children (goal-oriented, delayed). This mirrors how different age groups build habits — instant gratification at 5, deferred reward at 11.

05 · Key Screen Highlights

Three screens that carry the whole experience

Every design decision in these three screens was traced back to the motivations and mental models of each user group — not feature completeness.

06 · Two Visual Styles

Purple Shade & Orange Bloom

Two complete visual identities were developed — same UX structure, different emotional register. Parents could choose the theme that matched their child's personality, increasing ownership and daily engagement.

Style A — Purple Shade

"The overall style and colour conveys a vibrant, positive energy. The goal is to let both kids and parents feel hopeful and energised when using the app." Purple communicates imagination and creativity — ideal for children who lean toward fantasy and adventure themes.
